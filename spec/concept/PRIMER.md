# Space Prince — The Primer

*The player-facing copy in which the game explains itself — what astrology is, and what you are playing.*
*Two pieces: a short **framing** shown at mint, and the full **codex** summoned on demand.*

**Role.**
The codex is the game's deepest, most explicit explanation of itself, and doubles as a **standalone explainer** outside the game (a landing page, or something handed to someone before a demo).
It is **summoned, never a gate.** The first run is carried by experience, not by this page: the framing (below) states the stakes in a few lines, the first-ten-minutes arc (`spec/v2/ONBOARD.md`) teaches by play, and concepts arrive just-in-time — the unlock ceremony, the first house-node, the inspect "i" (`SCREENS.md §3.6.1`).
The foothold principle holds (`VIBES.md`, "offer a foothold; never force a lesson"): a player who never opens the codex loses nothing the game won't teach by being itself.
Placement of both pieces is a small open question for `SCREENS.md` (the framing at the mint / first-arrival step; the codex reachable from the Title).

**Voice.**
Spare, confident, unhurried — Space Prince explaining itself, not a textbook.
Assumes no astrology. Introduces every term on first use.
Stays above the named mechanics — *testimony, affliction, combustion, Resolve, fortune* are taught later, in play.

## The framing — shown at mint

*A few lines: the minimum that creates intent and stakes.*

```
Astrology is one of humanity's oldest systems of knowledge. For four millennia, astrologers have been making sense of the stars.

Space Prince is a game about astrology. But it's unlike others you've played.

Instead of playing **as a character**, you play as **yourself** — your birth chart is your stats sheet. Instead of getting stronger and dominating others, you play by **creating and relieving tension.**

The goal isn't to win, but to better understand yourself.

To begin, **cast your chart** and create a globally unique token: your Space Prince. Nobody else can play as you, because **nobody else is you.**

All of your games will be permanently recorded and reflected in your Prince's visual appearance. The video game save file turned art object.

Are you ready?
```

## The codex — summoned on demand

### What astrology is

Long before it was horoscopes, astrology was the oldest system humans built for reading a world in motion — a way of asking, of any moment: *what kind of time is this, and how will things tend to go?*

You do not need to believe it.
You need to learn to read it.

For four thousand years it has been refined:

- **Babylon** watched the sky because it was the largest stable pattern anyone could see, and read it for the fate of the city — when to plant, when to go to war. The twelve signs and the seven moving lights were named here.
- **The Greeks** turned the sky on the single person. They drew the **natal chart**: a map of the heavens at the exact moment and place of a birth — no longer the fate of a city, but the capacities of one individual, and the roles they might come to occupy.
- **Medieval** astrologers — Persian, Arab, European — made it a precise instrument, used to weigh kings and heirs and to time decisions. It is from this world that a *prince* is something a chart could describe.
- **The modern age** turned it inward. The chart became less a forecast of fate than a mirror of the self — a language of character, tension, and becoming.

Sky, then person, then instrument, then mirror.
Space Prince uses all four.

### What a chart is made of

A chart has four parts.

- **Seven planets** — the lights the ancients could follow with the naked eye: Sun, Moon, Mercury, Venus, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn. Each is a way of acting — a will, a mood, a kind of force.
- **Twelve signs** — the band of sky the planets travel through. The sign a planet stands in colors *how* it acts.
- **Twelve houses** — the domains of a life: the self, work, others, what is hidden. *Where* a planet sits is where it has its say.
- **Aspects** — the angles planets make to one another, by which they strengthen or strain each other.

A chart is not a list of seven things.
It is a web.

### What you are playing

You mint a **Prince**: a single moment and place — a latitude, a longitude, a time — resolved into one natal chart of seven planets.
It is **globally unique**, and it is yours.
The position is not invented for you; it is recognized, once, and never again.

This chart is your character, your save file, and the artifact you own — the same object, all three.

You play by choosing which planet to send against what you meet.
That is the whole of the action: *who do you send, and when.*

There is no growing stronger.
The chart you are given is the chart you keep.
What changes across many runs is not your power but your reading of it — how well you come to know what you are, and how to carry it against what stands opposite.
The game is about attention and balance, not conquest.

And nothing resets.
Every run is kept, permanently, written into the world.
Each one leaves a single **star** in your sky — its light the measure of the care you brought to it.
Over time the sky fills: a record, in light, of how you paid attention.

The chart is already complete.
You are only beginning to read it.
